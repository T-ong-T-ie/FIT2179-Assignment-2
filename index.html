Climate Dynamics & Epidemic Proliferation

Dengue fever remains one of the most critical public health challenges in tropical Southeast Asia. This advanced epidemiological dashboard presents a critical, multi-dimensional visual evaluation of how ambient macro-climatic controls—specifically relative atmospheric moisture, temperature gradients, and solar ultraviolet intensity—synergistically manipulate vector proliferation and dictate the geometric scale of outbreaks across Malaysian states.

Phase II

State-Level Drift & Interactive Down-Sampling

While macro-level geographic overviews successfully isolate global spatial configurations, they inevitably compress local temporal fluctuations and seasonal ranking drift. This interactive analysis segment shifts our heuristic lens toward granular state-level comparisons. By implementing interactive down-sampling and user-driven filters, viewers can isolate single observational years or target specific state trajectories, strictly mitigating cognitive impairment caused by overlapping datasets.

Critical Data Profiling Justification

During the initial data integration audit, a severe data discontinuity was identified in the raw meteorological records: solar UV Index metrics were completely absent or incorrectly logged as flat zeros prior to 2014 due to past weather sensor limitations. To protect visual sanity and eliminate misleading graphical anomalies within the Vega-Lite engine, a rigorous temporal truncation was executed, narrowing our multi-dimensional timeline strictly to 2014–2019. This strategic wrangling guarantees immaculate telemetry completeness across all selected environmental metrics.

Phase III

High-Density Risk Modeling & Diagnostics

Unifying independent data streams into a single interactive matrix layer uncovers the true ecological boundaries governing disease spread. This terminal diagnostics layer shifts from exploratory tracking to empirical epidemiology verification. By treating state-years as independent points in a multivariate field, the visualization stress-tests environmental thresholds, revealing a stark inverse relationship between annual thermal limits and moisture coefficients, and showcasing how high temperatures dominate vector scaling.

Idiom 07: The Epidemiology Triangle - Multidimensional Interaction Space
X-Axis: Temperature | Y-Axis: Relative Humidity | Size: Outbreak Volume | Color: Territory Mapping (Revealing thermal dominance over vector acceleration).
Epidemiological Dominance Evidence: The emergent clustering provides a striking empirical revelation that completely refutes generic environmental assumptions. First, the upper-right quadrant remains entirely vacant, demonstrating the macro-climatological decoupling of extreme heat and peak relative humidity in Malaysian annual averages. Second, while the upper-left quadrant is heavily populated by smaller data marks (indicating that high relative humidity alone under cooler monsoon profiles suppresses outbreak scale), the expanded, critical bubble marks heavily aggregate within the lower-right quadrant. This provides definitive proof of thermal acceleration: elevated temperatures (exceeding 27.5°C), even when paired with lower seasonal relative humidity, significantly shorten the extrinsic incubation period (EIP) of the dengue virus, triggering severe epidemic escalations.
